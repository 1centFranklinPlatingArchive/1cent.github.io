1¢ Franklin Issue of 1857-61, PLATE 5.
Position 70L5, (Scott #24 var)
Scott #24 Blue,
Type Va, Relief C
Issued only perforated.

This is part of a vertical strip of 3 consisting of 70L5, 80L5 and 90L5.

The images below are from a late or worn impression of the stamp. To study an early impression of this same position, See 70L5 early impression here. The violet arrows below are marks of interest and may not be consistent or reliable for plating. The red arrows indicate the remains of the left full plume that was not completely burnished from the plate.
Figure 2. (Below) Blurry vertical line as shown in the Neinken plating diagrams cutting across Ornaments O, P and Q.

Figure 3. (Below) Blurry smear between and below the "E" and "C." The violet arrows point out a horizontal scratch cutting into the "N" of ONE. This is not shown on the plating diagrams. The red arrows indicate the remains of the left full plume that was not completely burnished from the plate.
