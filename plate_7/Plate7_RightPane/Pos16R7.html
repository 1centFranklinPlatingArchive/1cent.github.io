1¢ Franklin Issue of 1857-61, PLATE 7.
Position 16R7, (Scott #24)
Scott #24 Blue,
Type V, Relief B
Issued only perforated.

Part of a horizontal pair consisting of 15R7 and 16R7.
Figure 1. (Below) Faint blur of color in Ornaments E, F and G as illustrated in the Neinken plating diagram. This plating mark is ill defined and probably fades quickly as the plate wears.

Figure 2. (Below) Faint blur of color in Ornaments O, and P as illustrated in the Neinken plating diagram. This plating mark is also ill defined and probably fades quickly as the plate wears.

Figure 3. (Below) Small dot of color above the interior unnamed ornament inside Ornaments AB not illustrated in the Neinken plating diagram but confirmed here as consistent. This dot appears on clearly-printed copies of at least 3 adjacent positions, 14R7, 15R7, and 16R7.
Confirming images courtesy of Mark and Sandy Friedman.

Figure 4. (Below) Three dots/dashes of color extending out from Ornament R and a dot to the right of Ornament Q not illustrated in the Neinken plating diagram but confirmed here as consistent.
Confirming images courtesy of Mark and Sandy Friedman.

Figure 5. (Below) The plating diagram illustrates a line of color in the O of ONE.
Confirming images courtesy of Mark and Sandy Friedman.
