1¢ Franklin Issue of 1857-61, PLATE 7.
Pos 9L7, (Scott #24)
Scott #24 Blue,
Type V, Relief A
Issued only perforated.

EYE CURL VARIETY

"Eye curls" occur on Positions 3L7 to 10L7 and 1R7 to 3R7.
Courtesy of Dick Celler
Figure 1. (Below) The famous "eye curl" first reported by the late Morris Fortgang. "Eye curls" occur on Positions 3L7 to 10L7 and 1R7 to 3R7. Wherever the eye curl occurs on a position, there is also a marking which looks like an inverted comma under the "T" in POSTAGE.

Figure 2. (Below) Faint vertical line inside of Ornament F/G as shown in the plating diagrams.

Figure 3. (Below) Faint vertical blur of color inside of Ornament A/B as shown in the plating diagrams.

Figure 4. (Below) The upper right ornaments. The plating diagrams illustrate a faint plating mark at this location.

Figure 5. (Below) Plating marks at Ornaments O, P/Q as shown in the plating diagrams.
