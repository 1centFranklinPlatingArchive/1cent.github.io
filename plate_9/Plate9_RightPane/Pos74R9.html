1¢ Franklin Issue of 1857-1861, PLATE 9
Position 74R9, (Scott #24)
Scott #24 Blue,
Type V, Relief D
Issued only Perforate.

FAINT CURL IN HAIR VARIETY

Part of a block of four consisting of 63R9, 64R9, 73R9 and 74R9.

It is interesting to note that the D reliefs coming from the 8th horizontal row (71R9 thru 80R9 and 77L9 thru 80L9) have blobs of color in the O and T of POSTAGE. Neinken notes this only on 72R9, 78R9, 80R9 and 80L9 but not the other positions.

This position is not illustrated in the Neinken plating diagrams and is noted as not having plating marks. Almost every position from Plate 9 has some identifing feature and careful examination of multiple patients will most likely reveal some plating feature.

FIGURE 1. (Below) Heavy dot of color below and between the C and E of CENT not illustrated in the Neinken plating diagrams but confirmed here as consistent.

FIGURE 2. (Below) Small dot of color in the shoulder not illustrated in the Neinken plating diagrams but confirmed here as consistent.

FIGURE 3. (Below) Small dots of color in the shoulder above the N of ONE and an additional dot tp the left of Ornament G/H not illustrated in the Neinken plating diagrams but confirmed here as consistent.

FIGURE 4. (Below) The faint CURL IN HAIR plating mark as illustrated in the plating diagram. This plating mark also appears in 34R9 and most dramatically on 33R9.

FIGURE 5. (Below) Right pane D reliefs coming from the 8th horizontal row (71R thru 80R) have blobs of color in the O and T of POSTAGE. 74R9 also has a dot in the P. Neinken notes these features only on 72R9, 78R9 and 80R9 but not the other positions.
