1¢ Franklin Issue of 1851-1857, PLATE 4
Position 77R4
Blue,
Type IIIa, Relief D
Issued both imperforate and perforate.

Plate 4 changed it's appearance as many plating marks faded away due to plate wear, and new plating marks developed over the life of the plate. One of the most annoying aspects of trying to identify Plate 4 positions is the appearance and disappearance of these plating marks.

PLATING NOTES: Neinken indicates this position as a Type III. This is in all probability an error as the lower curved line is not broken greater than 2mm in late printings. Closer scrutinty of other copies may find this position to be a TYPE CHANGLING. There is a faint vertical scribe/guideline between Ornament L/M. Faint horizontal blurry lines can be seen protruding from Ornaments Z and over Ornament Z1. These marks are similar in nature to Position 78R4. Plating marks in O, S and A of POSTAGE. Additionally there is a small DOT in the S of U. S.
