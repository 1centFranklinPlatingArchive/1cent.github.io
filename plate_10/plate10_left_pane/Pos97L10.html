1¢ Franklin Issue of 1857-1861, PLATE 10
Position 97L10
Scott #24 Blue,
Type V, Relief F
Issued only Perforate.


From the complete left pane of Plate 10.
Courtesy of Dick Celler

The heavy dot in the "C" of CENT has been confirmed as constant and is an excellent method for separating Plate 10 F Reliefs from other plates.
FIGURE 1. (Below) Plating marks in the upper left ornaments and heavy blur above the O and S of POSTAGE as illustrated in the Neinken plating diagram.

FIGURE 2. (Below) Plating marks in the upper right ornaments as illustrated in the plating diagram.

FIGURE 3. (Below)A diagonal line of color protruding from Ornament H as illustrated in the plating diagram.

FIGURE 4. (Below) The heavy dot in the "C" of CENT has been confirmed as constant and also has been confirmed on Positions 91 thru 100R10. The plating diagrams do not indicate this mark. This dot was probably a bit of matter that attached itself to the transfer roller when the plate was being made. Also note the location of the 10th row GUIDE DOT in the lower right plume.
